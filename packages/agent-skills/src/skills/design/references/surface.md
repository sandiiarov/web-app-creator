# Surface

Surface defines how landing-page sections, proof objects, images, forms, and controls use edges, radius, and depth. Use it when the page's physical treatment changes. Flat composition is a complete surface decision.

## Choose a surface model

- **Flat:** spacing, alignment, and color carry hierarchy
- **Framed:** restrained rules and edges create structure
- **Layered:** a small number of surfaces rise according to role
- **Atmospheric:** bounded light and shadow contribute to a hero or brand moment

One model leads. Borders and shadows support layout rather than compensating for unresolved hierarchy.

## Give edges a job

A border can separate neighboring sections, contain a proof object or form, define a control, show focus or validation, or establish an editorial frame. Spacing and surface contrast often separate content without another line.

Use a small vocabulary of semantic edge colors and weights. Focus treatment remains visible and unclipped. Form fields use clear resting, focus, and invalid boundaries where those states exist, with text or icons supporting color.

## Use radius as physical language

Radius can feel precise, soft, tactile, formal, raw, or technical. Related surfaces share a coherent family, and nested corners remain visually related. Pills fit controls or brands that earn them rather than acting as the automatic CTA shape.

## Tie depth to role

Landing-page depth can distinguish floating navigation, a mobile menu, a featured proof object, a conversion panel, an image frame, or a genuinely interactive item. Larger surfaces can use broader and softer shadow; small controls use restrained depth.

Shadows share one light direction. Dark surfaces often communicate depth more clearly through value and quiet borders than through black blur. Animated depth corresponds to a real hover, focus, or open state.

## Restraint

Avoid borders around every text group, unrelated radius recipes, shadow on empty cards, simultaneous heavy border and shadow, full-page glows, and hover lift on static content. Accent rails, glass, and strong elevation gain value only when the page premise gives them a specific role.

Large blurred shadows, stacked filters, and full-viewport effects can create obvious rendering cost. Ambitious depth stays bounded to a meaningful area.

## Verification

Use screenshots to judge separation, layer order, radius consistency, light direction, and whether the surface treatment supports proof and action. Inspect source for reused surface values, focus and form boundaries, and reduced-motion handling when depth animates.

## Done when

- Every visible edge or layer clarifies structure, interaction, or art direction
- Radius, border, and shadow form one physical vocabulary
- Focus and form boundaries remain clear for real controls
- Flat areas remain flat where elevation adds no meaning
- Surface treatment strengthens proof and conversion rather than generic decoration
